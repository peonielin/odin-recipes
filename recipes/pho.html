Pho

Home ingredients

Pho (pronounced "fuh") is a iconic Vietnamese noodle soup consisting of a rich, slow-simmered broth, flat rice noodles ( ), herbs, and tender meat (usually beef or chicken).

Ingredients

  • Broth: 2L beef or chicken broth, 500g beef bones or chicken carcass
  • Aromatics: 1 onion (halved), 4-inch piece of ginger (halved), 4 garlic cloves
  • Spices: 3 star anise, 4 whole cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • Seasoning: 2 tbsp fish sauce, 1 tbsp sugar, salt to taste
  • Noodles: 400g dried rice noodles (bánh phở)
  • Meat: 300g beef sirloin or brisket (thinly sliced) or cooked chicken
  • Toppings: Bean sprouts, fresh Thai basil, fresh cilantro, lime wedges, sliced chillies, hoisin sauce, sriracha

Steps

  1. Char Aromatics: Place onion and ginger cut-side down in a dry pan over high heat. Char for 5-7 minutes until blackened. This adds smoky depth to the broth.
  2. Toast Spices: In the same pan, toast star anise, cloves, cinnamon, and coriander seeds for 2-3 minutes until fragrant. Place in a spice bag or tea infuser.
  3. Build Broth: In a large pot, add broth, bones, charred aromatics, and spice bag. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for at least 1-2 hours (longer for deeper flavor). Skim off any foam.
  4. Season Broth: Remove aromatics and spices. Add fish sauce, sugar, and salt to taste. The broth should be savory, slightly sweet, and aromatic.
  5. Prepare Noodles: Cook rice noodles according to packet instructions. Drain and divide among serving bowls.
  6. Assemble Bowls: Top noodles with thin slices of raw beef or cooked chicken. Ladle hot broth over the top (the hot broth will cook the raw beef).
  7. Serve: Serve immediately with a plate of bean sprouts, Thai basil, cilantro, lime wedges, sliced chillies, hoisin sauce, and sriracha on the side.
